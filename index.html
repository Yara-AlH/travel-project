Why Stockholm?

Stockholm, the city of islands

The story goes that Stockholm was named 'The Venice of the North' for its archipelago of 14 islands, which makes it a unique and picturesque capital. Like Venice, Stockholm had its humble beginnings, and it's said that it was built on 14 islands that offered protection and a vibrant way of life. Although the moniker 'The Venice of the North' didn't become widely known, the city's connection to its island-dotted landscape has always been at its heart.

Even today, remnants of old Stockholm remain visible. Amid the bustling boutiques, charming waterfront cafés offering Swedish fika, and restaurants serving an array of delicious Nordic cuisine, you can still explore the city's archipelago, taking in the panoramic views, the colorful houses on the waterfront, and the expansive Baltic Sea on the horizon."

My favorite cafés in Stockholm

Vete Katten

From early morning to late evening, Vete-Katten is where you meet to start the day, talk, enjoy, gossip, think and date. Here you can find everything a classic patisserie can offer, plus a warm atmosphere and a pleasant environment.

Address:

Kungsgatan 55, 111 22

What I like about it

Vete-Katten's vintage charm is simply enchanting. The old-world ambiance, complete with elegant pastries and traditional Swedish treats, takes you on a delightful journey through time. It's a taste of history and culture in every bite.

Mr Cake

MR CAKE are a group of happy confectioners and bakers who are passionate about the love of the craft. Their products are selected with the finest ingredients, quality and taste are what people value most.

Address:

Rådmansgatan 12, 114 25

What I like about it

What I Like Most About It: Mr Cake is a haven for dessert lovers. The creative and artistic cake designs are not just visually stunning; they are incredibly delicious too. It's a place where you can indulge in decadent sweets that are almost too beautiful to eat.
